About

Kannur district derived its name from the location of its headquarters at Kannur town. The old name 'Cannanore' is the anglicised form of the Malayalam word Kannur. According to one opinion, 'Kannur' is a derivation from Kanathur, an ancient village, the name of which survives even today in one of the wards of Kannur Municipality. Another version is that Kannur might have assumed its name from one of the , deities of the Hindu pantheon, a compound of two words, Kannan (Lord Krishna) and Ur (place) making it the place of Lord Krishna. In this context, it is worth mentioning that the deity of the Katalayi Sreekrishna temple was originally installed in a shrine at Katalayi Kotta in the south eastern part of the present Kannur town.

Palakkayam Thattu

Palakkayam thattu is an Ootty like hill station located the western part of Kannur district where the tourists can chill out a day. Entry to this hill station is controlled by District Tourism Promotion Council with an entrance fee of 30 rupees for adults and 10 rupees for children. According to many travelers, Palakkayam thattu is special coolest place with natural breeze and mists and they feel like special romantic picnic spot. Visit this place either early morning or evening to explore it fully and your feel like touching the sky, feel the nature with fresh air.

Pakkasura Mala

Pakkasura mala is a scenic viewpoint near Kerala state. How to reach Pakkasura mala? Attapadi-Kottathara-Mettupalayam-Coonoor via Pakkasura mala. There will be a bunch of wild Bison looking at you on the way you go. Pakkasurahills are unexpored destination but tenting is not allowed. No restaurants or resorts are nearby so please make sure you bring your food and water exploring these virgin hills. It is about 35 km from Ooty and 40 km from Mettupalayam in the State of Tamil Nadu.
